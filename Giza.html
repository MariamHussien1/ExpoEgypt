1. Giza Pyramid Complex.

• Arabic : ( الاهرامات ).
• The Giza Pyramid Complex, also called the Giza Necropolis includes the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, along with their associated pyramid complexes and the Great Sphinx of Giza. All were built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of Ancient Egypt, between 2600 and 2500 BC. The site also includes several cemeteries and the remains of a workers village. The site is at the edges of the Western Desert, approximately 9 kilometres west of the Nile River

4. The Ancient City Of Memphis.

• Arabic : ( مدينة منف القديمة ).
• During its more than 3000 years of history, Memphis was an important political and religious centre, where the ancient Egyptian deity Ptah was venerated. It was also the city where the Pharaohs were crowned. Memphis was the capital of Lower Egypt before the two lands were unified around 3100 BCE by the Pharaoh Menes. At the height of its Golden Age it may well have been the most populated city in the world.
